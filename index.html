The World That is Finally Getting a Byte:

The effects in bringing technologic equity in the Global South

Harvard Business Review estimates that technology will create 12 million more jobs overall, which, combined with the fact that 90% of all jobs will require digital skills in 10 years, is devastating to countries with low adoption of technology. Technology is a double edged sword, and as technology improves exponentially over time, there is not much emphasis on its impacts to the developing world. This study hypothesizes that when introducing technology to the Global South and when addressing societal failures in using technology, there is a direct correlation in the increase of quality of life. Analysis of data found from reports and journals indicate an overall improvement towards quality of life in the Global South, specifically within the economies there. However, even if there is an overall improvement on quality of life, there are specific issues that need to be addressed individually for this to occur. This study uncovered both the positives and issues that technology brings in the Global South, which is to be used to foster the growth technology brings to the developing countries while preventing or minimizing such issues.
